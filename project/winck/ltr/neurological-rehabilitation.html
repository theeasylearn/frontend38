Neurological Rehabilitation

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession focused on human function, movement, and optimizing potential. It employs physical approaches to promote, maintain, and restore physical, psychological, and social well-being, considering variations in health status. A Neuro-physiotherapist specializes in treating patients with nervous system damage due to injury or disease. The nervous system, which is highly complex, includes the brain, spinal cord, and nerves that connect to the face, body, arms, and legs.
Who May Need Neurological Rehabilitation?

Patients experiencing the following functional difficulties may benefit:

  1. wallowing (dysphagia)
  2. Eating
  3. Washing and/or dressing
  4. Toileting
  5. Communicating
  6. Getting in and out of bed/chair/car
  7. Driving
  8. Walking
  9. Negotiating stairs

Movement may become awkward, painful, or difficult to control, making everyday activities challenging and potentially leading to a loss of independence. A Neuro-physiotherapist applies their knowledge of movement, nervous system function, and learning techniques to help patients overcome unique challenges effectively.
