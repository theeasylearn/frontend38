Pav Bhaji

Pav Bhaji is a popular Indian street food that consists of a spicy vegetable mash (bhaji) served with soft buttered bread rolls (pav). Here's a detailed recipe for making Pav Bhaji:

Ingredients

For Bhaji:

  1. 2 tablespoons oil
  2. 2 tablespoons butter
  3. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  4. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  5. 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  6. 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  7. 2 large tomatoes, finely chopped
  8. 1 cup green peas (fresh or frozen)
  9. 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  10. 1 cup cauliflower florets, boiled and mashed
  11. 1/2 cup carrot, finely chopped and boiled
  12. 1/2 cup bell pepper (capsicum), finely chopped
  13. 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  14. 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  15. 1 tablespoon pav bhaji masala
  16. Salt to taste
  17. 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  18. 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  19. 1/4 cup water (as needed)

For Pav:

  1. 8 pav (soft bread rolls)
  2. 2 tablespoons butter
  3. 1 teaspoon pav bhaji masala
  4. Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)

For Garnish:

  1. Finely chopped onions
  2. Lemon wedges
  3. Fresh coriander leaves
  4. Extra butter

Instructions

Preparing the Bhaji:

Heat oil and butter: In a large pan or skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of oil and 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat.
Add cumin seeds: Once the butter melts and oil is hot, add 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Sauté onions: Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add ginger-garlic paste: Add 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste and the finely chopped green chilies. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell of the ginger-garlic paste disappears.
Cook tomatoes: Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they become soft and mushy.
Add vegetables: Add the boiled and mashed potatoes, cauliflower, chopped carrots, green peas, and bell peppers. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.

Spice it up: Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, pav bhaji masala, and salt. Mix everything well and cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.

Mash the bhaji: Using a potato masher, mash the vegetables to a coarse paste. Add water as needed to achieve the desired consistency.

Simmer: Let the bhaji simmer for 10-15 minutes on low heat, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking to the pan.

Finish with lemon and coriander: Add lemon juice and fresh coriander leaves. Mix well and cook for another minute. Adjust seasoning if needed.

Preparing the Pav:

Butter the pav: Slice the pav horizontally, but not all the way through. Heat a skillet or griddle and melt 2
tablespoons of butter on it. Sprinkle 1 teaspoon of pav bhaji masala over the melted butter.
Toast the pav: Place the pav on the skillet, cut side down, and toast until they are golden and
crispy.

Serving:

Serve hot: Serve the hot bhaji in a bowl, topped with a dollop of butter, chopped onions, fresh coriander leaves, and a lemon wedge on the side. Enjoy with pav: Serve the toasted pav alongside the bhaji. Enjoy your delicious Pav Bhaji!

Tips:

You can add more vegetables like beans or corn if you like. Adjust the spice levels according to your preference. For an extra rich flavor, you can add more butter to the bhaji while cooking. Pav Bhaji tastes best when served hot.

Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian dish consisting of a crispy fermented crepe made from rice and lentils, filled with a spicy potato filling. Here's a detailed recipe for making Masala Dosa:

Ingredients

For Dosa Batter:

  1. 2 cups rice
  2. 1/2 cup urad dal (split black gram)
  3. 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  4. 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  5. Salt to taste
  6. Water as needed

For Potato Filling:

  1. 4 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  2. 2 tablespoons oil
  3. 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  4. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  5. 1 large onion, finely chopped
  6. 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  7. 2 green chilies, finely chopped
  8. 10-12 curry leaves
  9. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  10. Salt to taste
  11. 2 tablespoons fresh coriander leaves, chopped

For Garnish:

  1. Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
  2. Butter (optional)

Instructions

Preparing the Dosa Batter:

Rinse rice and dals: Rinse 2 cups of rice, 1/2 cup urad dal, 1/4 cup chana dal, and 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds thoroughly under running water.

Soak: Soak the rice and dals in enough water for 4-6 hours or overnight.

Grind: Drain the soaked rice and dals, and grind them together with some water to a smooth batter. The consistency should be slightly thick but pourable.

Ferment: Transfer the batter to a large bowl, cover it, and let it ferment in a warm place for 8-12 hours or until it doubles in volume and becomes bubbly.

Add salt: Once fermented, add salt to taste and mix the batter well.

Preparing the Potato Filling:

Heat oil: In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat.

Add mustard and cumin seeds: When the oil is hot, add 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds and 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds. Let them splutter.

Sauté onions: Add the finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.

Add ginger-garlic paste and chilies: Add 1 teaspoon of ginger-garlic paste, finely chopped green chilies, and curry leaves. Sauté for a couple of minutes until the raw smell of the ginger-garlic paste disappears.

Add turmeric and potatoes: Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder and the mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for a few minutes.

Season and garnish: Add salt to taste and fresh coriander leaves. Mix well and remove from heat.

Making the Dosa:

Heat the tawa: Heat a non-stick tawa or griddle over medium heat. Once hot, pour a ladleful of dosa batter onto the tawa and spread it in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.

Drizzle oil: Drizzle a little oil around the edges of the dosa and cook until the bottom turns golden brown and crispy.

Add filling: Place a portion of the potato filling on one side of the dosa, fold the dosa over the filling, and serve hot.

Serving:

Serve hot: Serve the masala dosa with coconut chutney, sambar, and a dollop of butter (optional). Enjoy your delicious Masala Dosa!

Tips:

Ensure the batter is fermented well for crispy dosas. You can adjust the spice levels in the potato filling according to your preference. For extra crispy dosas, you can add a little rice flour to the batter before making the dosas.
